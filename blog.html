Assisted Death ‚Äì Mercy or Morality?

July 20, 2025

The debate over assisted death is one of the most profound ethical challenges of our time. From a philosophical standpoint, thinkers like Kant argue for the sanctity of life, while utilitarians like Mill might prioritize reducing suffering. In practice, countries like the Netherlands have legalized it under strict conditions, raising questions about autonomy versus societal norms. This post explores these tensions, drawing on real-world examples and personal reflections.
